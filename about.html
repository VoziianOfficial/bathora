About the Platform

Bathroom projects feel easier when the options are clearer.

Bathora was created for homeowners who want a more organized way to compare bathroom provider options before speaking with independent local companies.

Aggregator-first. Not a direct contractor.
01

Why Bathora exists

Built around comparison, not contractor claims.

Bathroom updates can involve many different scopes: layout changes, shower systems, tile, vanities, tubs, repairs, fixtures, and surface work. The challenge is not only choosing a style. It is also understanding which local providers may be relevant for the actual project.

Bathora keeps that process focused. The platform helps homeowners sort project categories, request provider options, and move toward conversations with independent companies that may serve their area.

Homeowner Guide

What makes a bathroom request easier to compare?

Clear requests help reduce back-and-forth. Bathora encourages homeowners to describe the project in practical, provider-friendly language without making direct service claims.

01

Current condition

Mention leaks, outdated fixtures, poor storage, accessibility needs, or damaged tile.

02

Desired outcome

Clarify whether you want a full remodel, focused repair, modern shower, new tub, vanity, or flooring.

03

Decision factors

Think about materials, cleaning needs, timeline, budget range, and who will use the bathroom most.

What Bathora is

An independent matching platform

Bathora helps homeowners compare bathroom service categories and connect with local provider options where available.

What Bathora is not

Not a bathroom contractor

Bathora does not perform bathroom remodeling, repair, installation, or construction work directly.

What homeowners should do

Verify before hiring

Homeowners should review provider licensing, insurance, quotes, scope details, timing, and written terms before hiring.

A more structured path from idea to provider conversation.

Why It Feels Different

Clearer comparison before the first provider conversation.

Bathora gives homeowners more context before they submit a request. Instead of sending a vague “bathroom project” message, the site separates service types, explains what details matter, and reminds visitors to verify each independent provider before hiring.

This helps the experience feel calmer, more organized, and more trustworthy while keeping the aggregator model clear: Bathora helps with matching, not direct bathroom work.

Verification Checklist

Questions to ask before choosing an independent bathroom provider.

License and insurance

Ask for proof that the provider can legally perform the type of work required in your area.

Written estimate scope

Confirm what is included, what is excluded, and how changes are handled.

Warranty and payment terms

Review deposit requirements, milestone payments, product warranties, and labor guarantees.
